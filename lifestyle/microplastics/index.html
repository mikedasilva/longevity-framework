Microplastics

Microplastics are tiny plastic particles that have become ubiquitous in our environment and can pose potential health risks. It's important to minimize our exposure to these particles. This page will break down some guidelines to follow for each room in your house to help reduce your contact with microplastics and create a healthier living space.

Kitchen

Generally, avoid any plastic or chemical coming into direct contact with heat or anything to be consumed (including preparation and storage).

  • Solid wood or steel cutting boards
  • Stainless steel or cast iron cookware (ie: pots, pans, etc.)
  • Stainless steel eating and cooking utensils (ie: tongs, forks, knives, spoons, spatulas, etc.)
  • Reverse osmosis water filter system (for drinking and cooking water)
  • Glass storage containers, bottles, and jars (lids might be plastic so don't heat with them on)
  • Stainless steel blender container (Vitamix has one)
  • Stainless steel or glass measuring cups and spoons
  • Stainless steel strainer and colander
  • Stainless steel or glass mixing bowls
  • Ceramic dishware
  • Ceramic or glass cups
  • Medical grade silicone baking spatulas (not the same as what's labelled as food grade)
  • Glass, cast iron, ceramic, or stainless steel baking dishes/pans
  • Stainless steel or glass kettle
  • Stainless steel or glass coffee maker
  • Glass bottle for your sodastream system
  • Stainless steel lined tub in dishwasher
  • 100% cotton or linen towels
  • Plastic alternatives for cleaning (ie: sponges, scrubbers, etc.)
  • Prepared foods, condiments, spices, etc. that come in glass containers

Bedroom

The goal here is to have clean air and avoid breathing in any microplastics from sleep wear or bedding.

  • Use 100% cotton, linen, or wool sheets and blankets
  • Wool or down filled duvets and pillows
  • Hepa filter air purifier
  • 100% cotton or linen curtains
  • 100% cotton or linen sleepwear
  • Wood flooring
