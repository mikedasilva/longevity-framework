Ingestion

Cooking

We end up ingesting a lot of toxins just by using products that may leave a residue on or leech material into the food that we prepare with them. To minimize this, there are certain products and types of materials that are best to use when coming into contact with anything that we ingest.

Suggestions

  • non-coated stainless steel or cast iron cookware (frying pans, baking pans, pots)
  • glass or ceramic bowls, plates, cups, etc
  • stainless steel cooking utensils (spatulas/turners/flippers, ladles, anything that comes into contact with heat)
  • natural, non-toxic dish cleaning soaps and detergents
  • never use plastic for cooking or heating anything

Why

Anything non-stick usually means there is a coating to make it not stick. Over time, this coating wears off and ends up in whatever is being prepared in/with it. Other items that are made of plastic will also over time wear and end up on the food being prepared (especially if in contact with heat). Rule of thumb - stainless steel or glass over plastic for everything in the kitchen. Visit the EWG to evaluate dish cleaning products for their toxicity as they may leave a residue that also ends up in your food. Something to note is that when something says microwave safe (ie: plastic items), it simply means it won't fall apart in the microwave. It doesn't mean it's actually safe healthwise.
