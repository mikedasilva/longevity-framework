Sleep Quality

While there are numerous variables that can influence the quality of our sleep, the goal here is to simply highlight the basics that have strong evidence of positive impact on sleep quality.

Suggestions

  • Avoid alcohol - Alcohol destroys sleep quality â€“ it reduces REM sleep, lowers heart rate variability, raises your resting heart rate, increases body temperature, and increases the number of times you wake up throughout the night
  • Be consistent - Go to bed and wake up at the same time every single day
  • Sleep pressure - Avoid blue light in the evenings. Wear blue-blocking glasses and turn on night mode on electronic devices to cut out blue light.
  • Partake in sleep-enhancing practices - sauna, hot tub, hot baths, etc.
  • Body temperature - Sleep with a cooler temperature keeping your core cooler and your feet and hands warmer
  • Darkness - Keep your room dark
  • Choose mattresses, pillows, bedding, and other items in your room free from VOCs

Research

  • Dr. Mathew Walker's book - Why We Sleep
  • Mattresses could emit higher levels of VOCs during sleep

Diet

The food we eat can have a huge impact on our health and lifespan. There's plenty of evidence that indicates certain foods can improve various aspects of our lives from cognitive function, digestion, inflammation, cancer prevention and many others.

You can use apps such as Yuka to help make better buying decisions. Generally, avoid processed foods, products with added sugars, preservatives, and ingredients you don't know.

As more research becomes available, the following lists will adapt to focus on what the science shows has a positive impact and what is known to have a negative impact and should therefore be avoided.

Stress Management

Stress can have an extremely negative impact on our short and long-term health. Having mechanisms for dealing with and avoiding stress will help minimize the negative affect it can have on our body.

Methods to reduce stress hormones (ex: cortisol)

  • yoga
  • mindful breathing
  • music

Lifestyle Choices

So many lifestyle choices can affect your overall health. There's a few categories that are of particular interest:

  • ingestion - topics include cooking & food storage, drinking water, etc.
  • inhalation - covers the air in our environment, smoking, etc.
  • topical - includes what we rub, spray, or use on our body
  • Other

Considering these factors when making decisions on products to use and our surroundings is an easy way of quickly assessing the impact on our body and long-term health.

Screening and Monitoring

An important aspect of living a long, healthy life is screening and using available technology to monitor our bodies.

Screening

The Longevity Framework provides helpful information around prevention of diseases leading to shortened lifespan or poor quality of life, but frequent screening adds additional layers of protection. Some screenings that can be performed are:

  • 4Kscore
  • Colonoscopy
  • Prostate-specific antigen (PSA)
  • Mammography

Research

  • Canadian Urological Association recommendations on prostate cancer screening and early diagnosis
  • Navigating the complexities and nuances of cancer screening

Monitoring
